About

Published on March 22, 2018

Hi there, I’m Riku.

I’m an Assistant Research Fellow at the University of Otago, working on projects geared towards Bioinformatics. I have worked with a variety of data during my Masters and my current job, including microarrays, RNA-seq, genotype data, and methylation microarray data.

I think I’m pretty good with using terminal and programming, but I’d say I’m better at using Google. I mainly work on a Mac/Unix computer (sshing into a RHEL server, or a NeSI computer), and have Arch Linux running at home. No, I don’t use Windows – ditched that shite of an OS since 2014 and I’m never going back.

I’ve got a bad habit where, when I get interested in something, I really get into it until I get a good idea of how it works or become (relatively) good at it. Past examples include: Vim, LaTeX, and this website/blog (Hugo). So if you find something cool or interesting, let me know ASAP.

Anyhow, I hope you enjoy reading some of my posts, and if you learn something new from it, that’ll make my day.
